Reverse Bucket List

This is a concept that is definitely bookselling (e.g. by Arthur Brooks). I am not fully convinced but let's play along. The concept states that satisfaction is haves/wants, so reducing the wants still improves satisfaction — and other mathematical formulas applied to emotions like Happiness = Enjoyment + Purpose + Satisfaction. They often quote the Dalai Lama: "We need to learn how to want what we have, not to have what we want."

The rules:

  • It must be something I won't do.
  • It must be something that seems fun or rewarding to others.
  • Cheat on my wife
  • Become a smoker
  • Look into paranormal / religion for answers – i.e. stop following logic / science
  • Buy stuff to show off
  • Heal from imposter syndrome (fucking annoying but keeps me grounded)
